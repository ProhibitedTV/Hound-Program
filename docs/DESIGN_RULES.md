# Hound Program Design Rules

## Visual Language

- Alien animal, not robot dog.
- Spacecraft skull, sensor slits, no cute face.
- No obvious dog ears, dog eyes, or dog mouth.
- The machine should look coherent, functional, and unsettling.
- Every visible panel should appear to protect, vent, sense, or actuate.

## Engineering Language

- Alpha first. Fleet always in mind.
- Keep the locomotion platform boring and reliable.
- Treat the external shell as a replaceable identity layer.
- Use field replaceable units wherever possible.
- Prefer common fasteners, standard connectors, and repeatable manufacturing steps.

## Printability Rules

- Avoid large unsupported overhangs.
- Split parts instead of forcing ugly supports.
- Use boring strong brackets under expressive shells.
- Keep armor non-load-bearing until proven otherwise.
- Design for Kobra 2 Max realities: PLA+/Tough PLA first; PETG is not the default material.

## Safety / Public Interaction

- The hounds may look uncanny, but behavior should be predictable and controlled.
- Emergency stop must exist before public movement tests.
- Public-facing behavior should be memorable, not hazardous.
